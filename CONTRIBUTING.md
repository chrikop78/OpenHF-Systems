# Contributing to OpenHF-Systems

Thank you for your interest in SkyLink-AI-Frequency.

This project is intended as an open, community-driven space for ideas, discussion, technical thinking, and future-oriented experimentation in the field of amateur radio, SDR, signal processing, and AI-assisted communication concepts.

## Who can contribute

Contributions are welcome from:
- radio amateurs
- AI developers
- SDR enthusiasts
- signal processing developers
- technically interested contributors
- anyone with constructive ideas, questions, criticism, or relevant experience

## What kind of contributions are welcome

Examples include:
- conceptual ideas
- technical discussion
- SDR-related experience
- AI / signal-processing thoughts
- regulatory perspectives
- documentation improvements
- project structure suggestions
- future implementation concepts

## Important regulatory note

Anyone who wants to participate in technical implementation, testing, or on-air experimentation must independently review and comply with the applicable national legal and regulatory requirements of their own country.

Participation in discussions or conceptual work does not replace personal responsibility for legal compliance.

## Project mindset

OpenHF-Systems is intended as:
- open
- transparent
- technically curious
- constructive
- respectful
- community-oriented

This project is not intended to become a place for destructive arguments, ego conflicts, or careless technical experimentation outside applicable legal frameworks.

## How to contribute

You can contribute by:
- opening an issue
- suggesting improvements
- sharing technical perspectives
- proposing documentation changes
- participating in future development discussions

## Current stage

This repository is currently an early public starting point for the project idea.

Structures, documentation, and participation workflows will evolve step by step.
